What is E-Waste?

Electronic waste, or more commonly known as E-Waste, refers to electronic products that ceased to have any value to their users. These electronic products can be new or old as long as it is being discarded by its owner, it becomes an electronic waste. The 6 main categories of e-waste includes:

Lamps

Small equipments

Screen & Monitors

Small IT & telecom equipments

Temperature exchange equipments

Large equipments

E-Waste in Singapore
Do you know how much E-Waste is being generated in Singapore? A staggering 60,000 tonnes of E-Waste is being generated annually in Singapore. To put this into perspective, that number adds up to about 70 mobile phones per person! Imagine that!

Why is E-Waste dangerous?
These discarded electronic products contains batteries or plugs which contains toxic and hazardous contaminants that pose a severe risk to both human and environmental health. These risks include cancer, neurological damage and climate change.

What is being done?
Singapore has established an initiative called Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) framework where producers of covered electrical & electronic equipments will be responsible for the collection & proper treatment of e-waste. So what can we do as individuals?

Repair electronic equipments where feasible

For those electronic equipments that have not fully spoilt, instead of discarding it you can try repairing it by yourself, giving the equipment a new life.

Find opportunities for reuse

If the items are still in good working condition and you do not have any use for them, instead of discarding it, why not give it to someone else? You can also go an extra mile to donate these items to charities.

Recycle

Before you discard something, the first thought should always be "Can I recycle this?" Find e-waste collection areas near you by clicking

Look for eco-label products

While purchasing for electronic items, look for environmentally products that has Singapore Green Label.
